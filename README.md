## Project Overview

The Smell of Sunday is an interactive narrative experience developed for the Interactive Narratives course of the Master in Multimedia at FEUP.

The project explores memory, displacement, and emotional return through a linear story divided into interactive scenes and short gameplay moments.

Players follow the life of Isabella, a young woman who leaves her rural hometown in search of independence in the city of Porto, only to rediscover the emotional weight of the life she left behind.

---

## Logline

A young woman with hopes and aspirations of a life in a big city, bustling with energy, decides to move to Porto. As time goes on, she experiences the highs and lows of living alone and what it means to build a new life for oneself.

---

## Synopsis

Isabella grows up in a small village in Trás-os-Montes where Sunday meals fill the house with a warmth she does not yet know how to value. Restless and certain that life waits for her elsewhere, she leaves for Porto, builds a career, and trades full tables for empty kitchens.

Years later, the smell of something slow-cooked drifting from a restaurant stops her on a cold street and returns her, without warning, to everything she left behind.

Far from home, she begins to cook the recipes she never wrote down, gather people around a table, and live the things she once took for granted. What once felt like a place she needed to escape turns out to be the thing she has been building towards the whole time.

## Narrative Structure

The experience follows a linear narrative structure divided into three acts, enriched with interactive mini-games.

Each act includes:
- Narrative scenes
- Interactive gameplay segments
- Environmental storytelling
- Sound and visual transitions

---

## Gameplay and Interaction

The experience includes short interactive segments such as:
- Point and click exploration
- Drag and drop mechanics on desktop
- Tap based interactions on mobile devices
- Mini-games representing everyday actions such as cleaning, cooking, and memory recall

These interactions are designed to reinforce emotional immersion rather than challenge based gameplay.
